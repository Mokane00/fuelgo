CS 3432 Mini-Project — FuelGO Petrol Station Application

Project Overview

FuelGO — Digital Fuel Payment Platform for Lesotho

FuelGO is a full-stack petrol station service application designed to digitalise fuel transactions across Lesotho. The system enables customers to locate nearby petrol stations, pay for fuel digitally, earn loyalty rewards, and track spending — while giving station employees and administrators real-time operational control.

Problem Definition

Lesotho's petrol stations operate primarily with cash and manual record-keeping, leading to: long queues, inaccurate stock tracking, no customer loyalty programs, and limited business intelligence for station owners. FuelGO digitises the entire transaction lifecycle — from station discovery to digital payment, loyalty rewards, and management reporting.

Key Entities & Stakeholders
Customer
Registers, finds stations, pays digitally, earns loyalty points, manages vehicles, sets price alerts.
Employee
Monitors pumps at assigned station, manages pump status, views daily transactions and revenue.
Administrator
Full system access — manages stations, employees, fuel prices, customers and business reports.
Station
Physical petrol station with pumps, fuel types, location, operating hours and contact info.
Transaction
Records each fuel purchase — customer, station, pump, fuel type, litres, amount, payment method.
Loyalty
Customer loyalty programme — Bronze/Silver/Gold/Platinum tiers, points earned per transaction.

Customer Guide

Getting Started

  1. 1
    Open FuelGO in your browser. On the login page, click Create Account.
  2. 2
    Fill in your full name, email address, phone number, and a strong password (min. 8 chars, 1 uppercase, 1 number, 1 special character).
  3. 3
    Click Register. You will be automatically logged in and redirected to your Dashboard.
Your account is secure — passwords are hashed with bcrypt and never stored in plain text.

Paying for Fuel

  1. 1
    Go to Stations from the sidebar or use the map to find a station near you.
  2. 2
    Click Pay on any station card, or select a pump number at the station.
  3. 3
    Choose your fuel type, enter the amount in Maloti (M) or litres, and select your vehicle.
  4. 4
    Enter your card details (test card: 4242 4242 4242 4242, any future expiry, any CVV).
  5. 5
    Confirm payment. A digital receipt is emailed to you and loyalty points are credited instantly.

Managing Vehicles

  1. 1
    Go to My Vehicles from the sidebar or Dashboard.
  2. 2
    Click Add Vehicle, enter plate number, make, model, year and fuel type from the dropdown.
  3. 3
    To edit a vehicle, click the Edit (pencil) icon. To delete, click the Delete (trash) icon and confirm.

Loyalty & Rewards

  1. 1
    Every M 1 spent earns 1 loyalty point. Points accumulate across all transactions.
  2. 2
    Tier upgrades: Bronze (0 pts) → Silver (M500 spent) → Gold (M2,000) → Platinum (M5,000).
  3. 3
    Go to Loyalty to view available rewards and click Redeem to exchange points.

Price Alerts

  1. 1
    Go to My Profile and scroll to the Price Alerts section.
  2. 2
    Select a fuel type from the dropdown and enter your threshold price (e.g. M 15.00/L).
  3. 3
    Click Add Alert. You will receive a push notification when the price drops to or below your threshold.
Maximum 5 active alerts per account.
Admin Guide

Managing Stations (CRUD)

  1. 1
    Log in as admin (admin@fuelgo.co.ls). You will see the Admin Dashboard.
  2. 2
    Create: Click Stations in the sidebar, then + Add Station. Fill in name, district, location, contact and hours. Click Save Station.
  3. 3
    Find/Search: Use the search bar above the stations table. Type a name or district and click Find.
  4. 4
    Edit: Click the Edit button on any row. Update fields and click Save Station.
  5. 5
    Delete: Click the trash icon and confirm the deletion prompt.

Running Reports

  1. 1
    Click Reports in the admin sidebar or go to reports.html.
  2. 2
    Select a report tab: Revenue by Station, Fuel Performance, Customer Loyalty, Station Ratings, or Transaction Detail.
  3. 3
    Use the Find button or search input to filter rows. Click column headers to sort.
  4. 4
    Click Export CSV to download the current report, or Print to print it.
Installation & Setup Guide

Prerequisites

  • Node.js 18+ — nodejs.org
  • MySQL 8.0+ — local or remote server
  • npm (comes with Node.js)
  • A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge)

Local Setup

  1. 1
    Clone/extract the project to your machine.
    cd fuelgo
  2. 2
    Install dependencies:
    cd backend && npm install
  3. 3
    Configure environment: Copy .env.example to backend/.env and fill in:
    DB_HOST=localhost
    DB_USER=root
    DB_PASS=yourpassword
    DB_NAME=fuelgo
    JWT_SECRET=any-long-random-string
    PORT=5001
  4. 4
    Initialise database & seed test data:
    node database/initDB.js && node database/seed.js
  5. 5
    Start the server:
    npm start — then open http://localhost:5001
Default demo accounts after seeding:
Customer: demo@fuelgo.co.ls / demo1234
Admin: admin@fuelgo.co.ls / admin123

Railway Deployment

  1. 1
    Install Railway CLI: npm install -g @railway/cli
  2. 2
    Login: railway login
  3. 3
    Link project: railway link
  4. 4
    Set environment variables in Railway dashboard (same keys as local .env), add a MySQL plugin.
  5. 5
    Deploy: railway up — live at your Railway domain.

Database Optimisation Notes

The schema includes 22+ indexes for optimised query performance: indexes on foreign keys, frequently filtered columns (date, role, status, district, tier), and composite indexes for common JOIN patterns. Transactions use conn.beginTransaction() / conn.commit() / conn.rollback() to ensure atomicity — if any step of a fuel payment fails, the entire operation is rolled back.
